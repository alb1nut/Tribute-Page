Dr Kwame Nkrumah

First prime Minister of the Gold Coast (Now Ghana)

Here's a time line of Dr. Kwame Nkrumah's life:

  • 1909 - Born in Nkroful, Gold Coast (now in Ghana) to a poor and illiterate family.
  • 1927 - He spent eight years in elementary school and became a pupil teacher after his completion at the age of 17years. When continuing his education in the training college on 1927, he lost his father that same year. The sudden death of his father made things difficult in terms of finances.
  • 1930 - With all these hardship he managed to graduate from college in 1930.
  • 1935 - Nkrumah had sought entry to Lincoln University some time before he began his studies there. he sent the school a letter noting that his application had been pending for more than a year. he enrolled despite lacking the funds for the full semester.[39] He soon won a scholarship that provided for his tuition at Lincoln University. He remained short of funds through his time in the US.[40] To make ends meet, he worked in menial jobs, including as a dishwasher.
  • 1939 -
    • He graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in economics and sociology
    • enrolled at Lincoln's seminary and at the Ivy League University of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia
  • 1942 - Nkrumah gained a Bachelor of Theology degree from Lincoln and the top student in the course.
  • 1944 - Nkrumah played a major role in the Pan-African conference held in New York , which urged the United States, at the end of the Second World War, to help ensure Africa became developed and free.
  • 1945 - Marxist C. L. R. James, in a letter introducing Nkrumah to Trinidad-born George Padmore in London, wrote: "This young man is coming to you. He is not very bright, but nevertheless do what you can for him because he's determined to throw Europeans out of Africa."
  • 1945 - Nkrumah co-founded the Pan African Congress, which became an influential voice against colonialism in Africa.
  • 1947 - Nkrumah returned to the Gold Coast and became general secretary of the newly-founded United Gold Coast Convention.
  • 1949 - Nkrumah split with the organization over its political objectives and formed the Convention People’s Party (CPP).
  • 1950 - Imprisoned by the British in 1950 for his political activities, Nkrumah was released in 1951 when his party won the general election in a landslide victory.
  • 1952 - He was elected prime minister
  • 1957 - As prime minister, Nkrumah led an aggressive campaign for independence and achieved it
  • 1960 - he formed a new government, the Republic of Ghana.
  • 1966 - Nkrumah’s government was overthrown by a coup d’état while he was on a trip to Beijing, China.
  • 1972 - Taking refuge in Guinea, Nkrumah spent the rest of his life in exile. He died in Bucharest, Romania

Our independence is meaningless unless it is linked with the total Liberation of Africa.
